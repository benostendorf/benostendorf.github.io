Benjamin Ostendorf

Postdoctoral fellow

Tavazoie lab, The Rockefeller University

I am a physician scientist working in the Tavazoie lab at The Rockefeller University. In my research, I focus on how the immune system interacts with cancer cells, and I apply wet-lab and computational methods to study these interactions.

I obtained an M.D. from the University of Freiburg in Germany, where I also completed my experimental doctoral thesis in Molecular Nephrology in the laboratory of Tobias Huber. I subsequently worked as a resident physician and research fellow in Internal Medicine at Charité in Berlin.

My main research interest lies in determining the factors that shape the interplay between immune and cancer cells. In particular, I study the impact of germline variation on individual patients’ anti-tumor immune responses. Additionally, I am invested in characterizing the mechanisms that enable cancer cells to evade immune responses and understanding the requirements of effective anti-tumor immunity.

I am currently looking for positions to establish a research group addressing these topics using experimental tumor immunology and computations systems biology approaches. Please contact me if my profile is of interest.
